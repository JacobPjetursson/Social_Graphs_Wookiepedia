'''Lok Durd''' was a [[Sexes|male]] [[Neimoidian]] arms developer who served as a [[general]] in the [[Separatist Droid Army]] during the [[Clone Wars]]. He was [[Defense of Maridun|defeated]] by [[Galactic Republic|Republic]] forces and was taken into Republic [[Prison|custody]].<ref name="Defenders">{{TCW|Defenders of Peace}}</ref> Durd, along with [[Nuvo Vindi]], were part of [[Lieutenant Commander]] [[Orson Callan Krennic|Orson Krennic's]] original plans for a [[prisoner]] exchange with the [[Confederacy of Independent Systems]] to secure the [[Rescue of Galen Erso|release]] of [[Galen Walton Erso|Galen Erso]], [[Lyra Erso]], and the baby [[Jyn Erso]] from imprisonment on [[Vallt]].<ref name="Catalyst">''[[Catalyst: A Rogue One Novel]]''</ref>

==Behind the scenes==
{{Quote|We decided that going with this sort of really big, heavy, rolly guy with an even bigger hat than Nute Gunray's would just&hellip;would work, would sort of sell that this guy was extremely flamboyant and very bossy and even more gluttonous than your average Neimoidian.|Series Designer [[Kilian Plunkett]], on modeling Lok Durd|Star Wars: The Clone Wars The Complete Season One}}
Lok Durd first appeared in "[[Defenders of Peace]]" from [[Star Wars: The Clone Wars (TV series)|''The Clone Wars'' television series]]. He was voiced by [[George Takei]], and was intended as a recurring villain, though this never came to pass.<ref name="NA">[http://www.newsarama.com/tv/010919-George-Takei.html NewsArama.com]</ref> Durd was never seen in any other episodes of ''The Clone Wars'', canceled in [[2013]] as part of a "new direction" for [[Lucasfilm Ltd.]]<ref name="New Direction">{{SW|url=news/a-new-direction-for-lucasfilm-animation.html|text=A New Direction for Lucasfilm Animation}}</ref>
