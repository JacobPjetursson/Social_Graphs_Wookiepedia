'''Chirpa''' was a [[Sexes|male]] [[Ewok]] who served as the [[chief]] of the [[Council of Elders (Ewok)|Council of Elders]] and Ewok [[Bright Tree tribe|tribe]] that lived in [[Bright Tree Village]] on the [[forest]] [[moon]] of [[Endor]]. During the [[Battle of Endor]], he allowed a [[Alliance to Restore the Republic|Rebel]] [[Pathfinders|strike team]], led by [[General]] [[Han Solo]], to become part of the tribe. The Ewoks also agreed to help the Rebels on their mission to destroy the [[Death Star's shield generator|generator]] powering the [[Death Star II|second Death Star's]] [[deflector shield]].<ref name="Episode VI" /> Chirpa was also [[Marriage|married]] to [[Ra-Lee]]<ref name="SW.com">{{SW|url=news/the-spooky-secrets-of-idws-tales-from-vaders-castle|text=The Spooky Secrets of IDW's ''Tales from Vader's Castle''|archivedate=20181022143116}}</ref> and had a daughter named [[Kneesaa]].<ref name="SW:AEYNtK">''[[Star Wars: Absolutely Everything You Need to Know]]''</ref>

After his father [[Buzza]], served as [[chief]], he gave his son the throne<ref name="Night of the Gorax">''[[Tales from Vader's Castle 4: Night of the Gorax]]''</ref> and Chirpa served for 42 seasons before the Battle of Endor which he was succeeded as Chief of the tribe by Kneesaa.<ref name="SW:AEYNtK" />

==Personality and traits==
Like all Ewoks, Chirpa was short. He had [[Color|brown]] [[eye]]s and a coat of gray and white [[Hair|fur]].<ref name="Episode VI" /> He was an experienced, fair-minded ruler.<ref name="Card Trader" />

==Behind the scenes==
Chief Chirpa was played by [[Jane Busby]] in the [[1983]] film [[Star Wars: Episode VI Return of the Jedi|''Star Wars'': Episode VI ''Return of the Jedi'']], the final installment of the ''[[Star Wars]]'' [[original trilogy]].<ref name="Episode VI" /> Originally, Chief Chirpa's name was Chief Chiepa in the early script incarnations of ''Return of the Jedi''.<ref name="Former DB">{{DB|character|chiefchirpa|Chief Chirpa}}</ref>

In [[2015]], [[Chuck Wendig]], author of the novel ''[[Aftermath]]'', expressed interest in potentially writing stories about [[Omi]], [[Malakili]] or Chirpa. However, he ultimately chose to write about Malakili instead in his sequel novel ''[[Aftermath: Life Debt]]''.<ref name="Chuck Wendig's War">{{InsiderCite|160|Chuck Wendig's War}}</ref>
